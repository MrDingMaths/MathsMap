# **Mathematics K--10**

## **Implementation for K--2 from 2023 and 3--10 from 2024**

**Mathematics for K−2**

The new syllabus must be taught in Kindergarten to Year 2 in all NSW
primary schools from Term 1, 2023.

**Mathematics for 3−10**

The new syllabus is to be taught in Years 3 to 10 from 2024.

**2023** Plan and prepare to teach the new syllabus

**2024** Start teaching the new syllabus

School sectors are responsible for implementing syllabuses and are best
placed to provide schools with specific guidance and information on
implementation given their understanding of their individual
circumstances.

## **Outcomes and content for Stage 5**

### **Financial mathematics A**

#### **Outcomes**

A student:

- develops understanding and fluency in mathematics through exploring
  and connecting mathematical concepts, choosing and applying
  mathematical techniques to solve problems, and communicating their
  thinking and reasoning coherently and clearly **MAO-WM-01**

- solves financial problems involving simple interest, earning money and
  spending money **MA5-FIN-C-01**

**Related Life Skills outcomes:** MALS-FIN-01, MALS-FIN-02

#### **Content**

##### **Solve problems involving earning money**

- Solve problems involving wages given an hourly rate of pay including
  penalty rates for overtime, weekends and public holidays

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Example(s):**                                                       |
|                                                                       |
| Calculating the hourly rate of pay if a person worked a 4-hour shift  |
| and earned \$45 including a \$5 tip.                                  |
|                                                                       |
| ![Graph showing hours worked and money earnt. End of the bar graph is |
| shaded darker and labelled                                            |
| \$5.](media/image2.png){width="5.972222222222222in"                   |
| height="0.9835312773403324in"}                                        |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

- Calculate earnings from non-wage sources exploring commission, piece
  work and royalties

- Calculate weekly, fortnightly, monthly and yearly earnings assuming 1
  year = 52 weeks

- Calculate leave loading by finding a percentage of eligible normal pay

- Investigate sources of published tables or online calculators and use
  these to calculate the weekly, fortnightly or monthly tax to be
  deducted from a worker's pay under the Australian Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG)
  taxation system

- Determine annual taxable income by exploring allowable deductions and
  current tax rates

- Calculate net earnings after deductions and taxation

##### **Solve problems involving simple interest**

- Establish and use the formula $$I = Prn$$ to find simple interest
  where $$I = \ $$simple interest, $$P = \ $$principal,
  $$r = \ $$interest rate per time period and $$n =$$ number of time
  periods

- Apply the simple interest formula to solve problems related to
  investing money at simple interest rates, both algebraically and
  graphically

##### **Solve problems involving spending money**

- Calculate the cost of buying items on terms, by paying an initial
  deposit and making regular repayments

- Examine payment options involving *buy now*, *pay later* and
  investigate the costs associated with these schemes for purchasing
  goods

+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Example(s):**                                                  |
|                                                                  |
| Exploring options for a student wanting to buy a laptop valued   |
| at \$2700. They have the option to buy it using a company that   |
| offers a *buy now*, *pay later* option. Researching which        |
| companies offer this facility, how will the cost of the laptop   |
| be split into repayment amounts and what costs or penalties are  |
| associated with the purchase if the student cannot make their    |
| repayments on time?                                              |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+

- Examine the principles behind short-term loans involving small dollar
  amounts and compare borrowing costs associated with using these
  products

###  ******Financial mathematics B**

#### **Outcomes**

A student:

- develops understanding and fluency in mathematics through exploring
  and connecting mathematical concepts, choosing and applying
  mathematical techniques to solve problems, and communicating their
  thinking and reasoning coherently and clearly **MAO-WM-01**

- solves financial problems involving compound interest and depreciation
  **MA5-FIN-C-02**

**Related Life Skills outcomes:** MALS-FIN-01, MALS-FIN-02

#### **Content**

##### **Solve problems involving compound interest and depreciation**

- Examine compound interest for up to 3 time periods using repetition of
  the formula for simple interest

- Associate the calculation of the total value of a compound interest
  investment with repeated multiplication, using digital tools

+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Example(s):**                                                  |
|                                                                  |
| Modelling repeated multiplication with digital tools for a rate  |
| of 5% per annum, using 1.05 for each year.                       |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+

- Establish and use the formula $$FV = PV(1 + r)^{n}$$ to find compound
  interest where $$FV$$ $$=$$ future value of the investment,
  $$PV =$$ present value of the investment, $$r = \ $$interest rate per
  time period and $$n =$$ number of time periods

- Solve problems involving compound interest

+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Example(s):**                                                  |
|                                                                  |
| Using a *guess* and *refine* strategy to determine the number of |
| years required to have \$2000 from an investment of \$500 if     |
| interest is compounded quarterly at 4.8% per annum.              |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+

- Compare simple interest with compound interest in practical situations

- Use the compound interest formula to establish the depreciation
  formula $$S = V_{0}(1 - r)^{n}\ $$where $$S =$$ salvage value,
  $$V_{0} = \ $$initial value of the asset, $$r =$$ depreciation rate
  per time period and $$n =$$ number of periods

- Solve problems involving the depreciation of an asset
