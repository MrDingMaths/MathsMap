# Syllabus Content

**MAV-12-02** models, analyses and solves practical problems involving
functions and their transformations

**Modelling with functions**

- Model and solve problems involving periodic phenomena using
  transformations of the trigonometric functions $f(x) = \sin x$,
  $f(x) = \cos x$ or $f(x) = \tan x$, without the use of calculus

- Model and solve practical problems involving functions and their
  transformations, within the scope of the Mathematics Advanced course,
  using graphical and algebraic techniques

- Recognise that logarithmic scales are useful to represent a variable
  that ranges over a large set of values and explain when a logarithmic
  scale is suitable

- Use logarithmic scales to model and solve problems involving decibels
  (dB) in acoustics, seismic scales for earthquakes, magnitudes of stars
  and the pH scale in chemistry

    height="1.7716535433070866in"}A vertical spring is pulled down and
    then let go. It bounces back up and down again according to the
    equation $h\  = \ 12\ \cos\ t\  + \ 15$ where $h$ is the height of
    the spring in cm and $t$ is time in seconds. The graph of its motion
    is shown below for the first 10 seconds.

    a.  Describe the significance of the 15 in the equation.

15 is the mean, or equilibrium, position of the spring.

b.  What are the maximum and minimum heights of the spring?

Maximum = 15 + 12 = 27 cm

Minimum = 15 - 12 = 3 cm

c.  What is the height of the spring after $\pi$ seconds?

$$h\  = \ 12\cos\pi + \ 15\  = \ 3\ cm$$

d.  At what times will the spring be at its minimum height?

Minimum when $\cos\ t\  = \  - 1$

$t\  = \ \pi,\ 3\pi,\ 5\pi$, \... seconds

2.  The depth of water in Dolphin Bay varies according to the tides. The
    depth is modelled by the equation
    $x\  = \ 2\ \cos\left( \frac{\pi}{7}t \right)\  + \ 8$, where $x$
    metres is the depth and $t$ hours is the time since the last high
    tide. Last Saturday, it was high tide at 7 am.

    a.  How deep is the bay at high tide?

$$x(0)\  = \ 2\ \cos(0)\  + \ 8\  = \ 10\ metres$$

b.  How deep is the bay at low tide?

$$x(6)\  = \ 2\ \cos(\pi)\  + \ 8\  = \ 6\ metres$$

c.  When did the first low tide after 7 am occur?

Low tide when $\cos\left( \frac{\pi t}{7} \right) = \  - 1$

$$\frac{\pi t}{7} = \ \pi$$

t = 7 hours after 7 am = 2 pm

d.  At what time last Saturday morning was the depth 9 metres?

$$9\  = \ 2\ cos\left( \frac{\pi t}{7} \right) + \ 8$$

$$\cos\left( \frac{\pi t}{7} \right) = \frac{1}{2}$$

$$\frac{\pi t}{7} = \frac{\pi}{3}$$

t = $\frac{7}{3} = 2\frac{1}{3}$ hours after 7 am = 9:20 am

4.  The voltage, $V$ volts, supplied by an electrical outlet is
    described by the sine function\
    $V\  = \ k\ \sin\ at\  + \ c$, where $k$, $a$ and $c$ are constants,
    $t$ is measured in seconds and $k > 0$.

    a.  If the voltage, $V$, oscillates between -240 volts and 240
        volts, find the value of $k$ and $c$.

$$k\  = \ 240$$

$$c\  = \ 0$$

b.  If it has a frequency of 50 Hz (meaning 50 cycles per second), find
    the value of $a$.

Frequency = 50 Hz, so period = $\frac{1}{50}$ s

$$a\  = \frac{2\pi}{\frac{1}{50}} = \ 100\pi$$

c.  Determine the voltage $(V)$ function as a transformation of the sine
    function.

$$V(t) = 240\ \sin(100\pi t)$$

5.  A set of tidal waves has a maximum height of 20 m and a minimum
    height of 6 m.\
    The waves break every 10 seconds.

    a.  Find the equation of a sine function $y = k\sin(at) + c$ that
        describes the wave motion.

Mean position = $\frac{20\  + \ 6}{2}$ = 13 m

Amplitude = $\frac{20\  - \ 6}{2}$ = 7 m

Period = 10 s, so $B\  = \frac{2\pi}{10} = \frac{\pi}{5}$

$$y\  = \ 7\ \sin\left( \frac{\pi t}{5} \right) + \ 13$$

b.  Find the first 4 times that the waves reach their maximum height.

$$20 = 7\sin\left( \frac{\pi t}{5} \right) + 13$$

$$\sin\left( \frac{\pi t}{5} \right) = \ 1$$

$$\frac{\pi t}{5} = \frac{\pi}{2},\frac{5\pi}{2},\frac{9\pi}{2},\frac{13\pi}{2}$$

$$t\  = \ 2.5,\ 12.5,\ 22.5,\ 32.5\ seconds$$

c.  Find the first time that the waves reach their minimum height.

Minimum when $\sin\left( \frac{\pi t}{5} \right)\  = \  - 1$

$$\frac{\pi t}{5} = \frac{3\pi}{2}$$

$t\  = \ 7.5$ seconds

d.  When will the height of the waves be in the centre?

Centre height when $\sin\left( \frac{\pi t}{5} \right)\  = \ 0$

$$\frac{\pi t}{5} = \ 0,\ \pi,\ 2\pi,\ 3\pi,\ 4\pi,\ 5\pi,\ ...$$

$$t\  = \ 0,\ 5,\ 10,\ 15,\ 20,\ 25,\ ...\ seconds$$

6.  The height of the water, $h$ metres, in a bay varies over time and
    can be modelled by a cosine function of the form
    $h(t)\  = \ k\ \cos\ at\  + \ c$, where $k$, $a$ and $c$ are
    constants and $t$ represents the number of hours after midnight. On
    a particular day off the coast of South Australia, a high tide of
    4.5 metres occurred at 6:00 am and a low tide of 0.5 metre occurred
    at 12:00 pm.

    a.  Determine a model for the height, $h$ metres, of the water as a
        function of time, $t$ hours, since midnight by finding the
        values of $k$, $a$ and $c$.

$$k\  = \frac{4.5\  - \ 0.5}{2} = \ 2$$

$$c\  = \frac{4.5\  + \ 0.5}{2} = \ 2.5$$

Half-period = 6 hours, so period = 12 hours

$$a\  = \frac{2\pi}{12} = \frac{\pi}{6}$$

b.  Hence write the rule of $h(t)$.

$$h(t) = \ 2\ cos\left( \frac{\pi t}{6} \right) + \ 2.5$$

c.  At what time does the second high tide occur?

Next high tide: period = 12 hours after 6 am = 6 pm

    $T = 15\ \sin\left( \frac{\pi t}{12} \right) + 20$, where $t$ is the

    a.  Sketch the graph of the temperature, $T\ {^\circ}C$, inside the

$$Time\ at\  \geq 30{^\circ}C:\ 9.2\  - \ 2.8\  = \ 6.4\ hours$$

f.  It is known that the temperature inside the classroom follows the
    outside temperature by\
    3 hours, that is
    $T_{out} = 15\sin\left( \frac{\pi}{12}(t + 3) \right) + 20$. Sketch
    both graphs on the same axis above.

Same graph shifted left by 3.

g.  When are the temperatures the same and what temperature is this?

The peak of inside temperature is at $t = 6$

The peak of the outside temperature is at $t = 3$

As the outside temperature decreases, the inside temperature increases.

Since the two graphs are identical except for shifting, the rate of
change of temperature is symmetrical.

Therefore, temperatures will be equal at the midpoint $t = 4.5$ at
$T(4.5) \approx 33.86{^\circ}C$

algebraic method is to set the equations equal and solve using:

$if\sin X = \sin Y,\ then\ X = Y\ or\ X = \pi - Y$ ($y$-coordinates in
unit circle are equal in first or second quadrant)

Only the second case would yield a valid solution of $4.5$

>
> where $t$ is the time in hours since low tide.

Low Tide = 1.3 - 0.6(1) = 0.7 m

High Tide = 1.3 - 0.6(-1) = 1.9 m

    tides?

Time between two low tides = Period of equation $(n)$

$$\frac{2\pi}{n} = \frac{4\pi}{25}$$

$$\frac{n}{2\pi} = \frac{25}{4\pi}$$

$$n\  = \frac{25}{2}hours$$

c.  For how long between successive low tides will the depth of water be
    at least 1 metre?

$$Find\ t\ when\ d\  = \ 1$$

$$1.3\  - \ 0.6\ \cos\left( \frac{4\pi t}{25} \right)\  = \ 1$$

$$- 0.6\ \cos\left( \frac{4\pi t}{25} \right) = \  - 0.3$$

$$\cos\left( \frac{4\pi t}{25} \right) = \frac{1}{2}$$

$$\frac{4\pi t}{25} = \frac{\pi}{3},\frac{5\pi}{3}$$

$$t\  = \frac{25}{12},\frac{125}{12}$$

$$= \frac{100}{12}$$
