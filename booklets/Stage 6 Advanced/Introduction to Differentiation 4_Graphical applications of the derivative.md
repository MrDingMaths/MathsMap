# Syllabus Content

**MAV-11-06** interprets the meaning of the derivative and determines
the derivative of functions to solve problems

**Graphical applications of the derivative**

- Interpret $f(x)$ as increasing at $x = c$ when $f'(c) > 0$ and
  decreasing at $x = c$ when $f'(c) < 0$

- Describe the behaviour of a function at a point as stationary when the
  tangent at the point is parallel to the $x$-axis, and recognise that
  $f(x)$ is stationary at $x = c$ when $f'(c) = 0$

- Graph $y = f'(x)$ for a given graph of a function $y = f(x)$

- Numerically estimate the value of the derivative at a point on the
  graph of a power of $x$, with and without the use of digital tools

- Identify stationary points on the graph of a cubic function, and the
  values of $x$ for which the function is increasing and/or decreasing,
  by first calculating the derivative, and justify conclusions

Foundation

1.  A function is increasing in a domain if the gradient of the tangent
    is p.....................

positive

2.  A function is decreasing in a domain if the gradient of the tangent
    is n.....................

Negative

3.  When a function has a stationary point, the gradient of the tangent
    is ............

This can either be a t.................. point or a point of
i.....................

Zero, turning, inflection

Since $f(x)$ is strictly increasing for all $x$, it can cross the

And it must cross the $x$-axis at least once because it is strictly
