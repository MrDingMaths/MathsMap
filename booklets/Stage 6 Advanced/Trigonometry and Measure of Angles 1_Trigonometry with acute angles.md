# Overview

## Syllabus Content

**MAV-11-04** applies trigonometry to solve problems involving geometric
shapes

**Trigonometry with acute angles**

- Use Pythagoras' theorem to find the exact sine, cosine and tangent
  ratios for angles of $30{^\circ}$, $45{^\circ}$ and $60{^\circ}$

- Apply trigonometry to solve problems involving right-angled triangles
  in two dimensions, true and compass bearings, and angles of elevation
  and depression

17. From the ends of a straight horizontal road 1 km long, a balloon
    directly above the road is observed to have angles of elevation of
    57° and 33° respectively.\
    Find, correct to the nearest metre, the height of the balloon above

![A triangle with numbers and a few angles AI-generated content may be
incorrect.](media/Trigonometry and Measure of Angles 1_Trigonometry with acute angles/media/image31.png){width="1.5897440944881889in"

Draw a vertical line from the apex of the triangle to the base, this
represents the height $h$.

This vertical line splits the base into two sections, which we will
label $a$ and $b$.

$$\tan{57{^\circ}} = \frac{h}{a},\ \ \ \tan{33{^\circ}} = \frac{h}{b}$$

$$a = \frac{h}{\tan{57{^\circ}}},\ \ \ b = \frac{h}{\tan{33{^\circ}}}$$

$$a + b = 1\ km$$

$$\frac{h}{\tan{57{^\circ}}} + \frac{h}{\tan{33{^\circ}}} = 1$$

$$h\left( \frac{1}{\tan{57{^\circ}}} + \frac{1}{\tan{33{^\circ}}} \right) = 1$$

$$h = \frac{1}{\left( \frac{1}{\tan{57{^\circ}}} + \frac{1}{\tan{33{^\circ}}} \right)} = 0.45677\ldots$$

$\therefore$ 457 metres

# Angles of Elevation and Depression

- **Line of sight** is a straight line from your eye to an object.

- Angle of **elevation**: The angle you turn to look up from the
  horizontal.

- Angle of **depression**: The angle you turn to look down from the
  horizontal.

- The **horizontal** is a line parallel to the ground.

# Sine Rule

  - The **longest** **side** is opposite the **largest angle.**

  - The **shortest side** is opposite the **smallest angle.**
