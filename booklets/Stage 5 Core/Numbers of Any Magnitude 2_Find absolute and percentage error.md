# Overview

## Syllabus Content

**MA5-MAG-C-01** solves measurement problems by using scientific
notation to represent numbers and rounding to a given number of
significant figures

**Find absolute and percentage error**

- Determine the precision of a measuring instrument by finding the
  smallest division on the instrument

- Find the absolute error of measuring instruments
  ($absolute\ error = \frac{1}{2} \times precision$)

- Calculate the percentage error of a given measurement by applying the
  formula:
  $percentage\ error = \frac{absolute\ error}{measurement} \times 100\%$

  it can measure.

- Unless otherwise stated, it is the **smallest place value** of the
  stated measurement.

## Limits of Accuracy

- The **limits of accuracy** describe the possible values that a
  measurement could be, considering the inaccuracies of the measuring
  instrument.

![](media/Numbers of Any Magnitude 2_Find absolute and percentage error/media/image8.png)

  ---------------------------------------------------
  **Limits of Accuracy**
  ---------------------------------------------------
  $${Absolute\ Error = \frac{1}{2} \times Precision
  }{Lower\ Bound = Measurement - Absolute\ Error
  }{Upper\ Bound = Measurement + Absolute\ Error}$$

  ---------------------------------------------------

- To find the limits of accuracy:

1.  Identify the precision.

2.  Identify the absolute error.

3.  Find the upper bound.

4.  Find the lower bound.

# Percentage Error

- The **percentage error** compares the absolute error to the
  measurement.

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
                            **Percentage Error**
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   $$Percentage\ Error = \frac{Absolute\ Error}{Measurement} \times 100$$

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  Identify the **precision**. This is the smallest place value of the
    measurement.

2.  Identify the **absolute error**; the absolute error is half the
    precision.

3.  Calculate the percentage error using the formula.

### **Example C**alculate the percentage error of a measurement.

Foundation

1.  Vivaan measured the mass of an iPhone as 251 g.

    a.  Identify the precision.

1 g

b.  Calculate the absolute error.

0.5 g

c.  Find the limits of accuracy.

250.5 to 251.5 g

d.  Find the percentage error. Answer correct to two decimal places.

$$\frac{0.5}{251} \times 100 = 0.20\%$$

P: 0.1 kg

$$\frac{0.05}{49.6} \times 100 = 0.10\%$$

    a.  Find the limits of accuracy.

$$\frac{0.005}{2.71} \times 100 = 0.185\%$$
