# Significant Figures

- **Significant ­figures** are meaningful digits in a number.

- It's impossible to cut a piece of timber to a length of 1.333 333...
  m. 

- The digits after a certain figure, or digit, are meaningless.

<!-- -->

- **Rules for significant figures:**

  1.  The first significant figure is the first non-zero digit (reading
      from left to right).

  2.  All non-zero digits are significant.

  3.  Zeros between non-zero digits are significant.

  4.  Zeros on the end of a decimal are significant.

  5.  ![](media/Numbers of Any Magnitude 3_Estimate and round numbers to a specified degree of accuracy/media/image1.png){width="3.348611111111111in"
      height="1.5972222222222223in"}Zeros on the end of a whole number
      *may* be significant.\
      Treat them as significant until we learn scientific notation.

## Rounding to a Significant Figure

- To round to 𝑛 significant figures:

1.  Identify the $n$th significant figure.

2.  Determine the place value of this digit.

3.  Round to this place value.
