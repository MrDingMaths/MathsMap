Development

10. A postage stamp has an area of 5 cm^2^. How many postage stamps will
    cover an area of 1 m^2^?

$10000 \div 5 = 2000$ stamps

11. ![](media/image12.png){width="1.577470472440945in"
    height="0.9375in"}Consider the rectangle to the right.

    a.  Calculate the area of this triangle, giving answer in cm^2^.

21 cm^2^

b.  Convert your answer mm^2^.

21 cm^2^ $\times 10^{2} =$ 2100 mm^2^

c.  Redo the calculation by first converting 3 cm and 7 cm to mm, then
    calculate the area, giving answer in mm^2^.

30 mm x 70 mm = 2100 mm^2^

d.  When doing area calculations, does it matter if you first convert
    the lengths or convert the area at the end? Which do you prefer?

Students tend to make mistakes converting units of area, so Mr Ding
recommends you convert lengths before calculating area.

12. Calculate the area of a rectangle with dimensions 12 cm and 0.2 m.\
    Answer in an appropriate unit.

240 cm^2^

13. A student writes: "To convert cm² to m², I divide by 100 because
    there are 100 cm in a metre."\
    Explain the error. What should the student divide by?

There are 100 cm in 1 m, but 10 000 cm² in 1 m².

Each dimension scales by 100, so the area scales by 100² = 10 000.
Divide by 10 000.

14. Two students compare answers:

Aaliya says: 3.5 m² = 35 000 cm²

Ben says: 3.5 m² = 3 500 cm²

Who is correct? Show working to justify your answer

Aaliya. 3.5 × 10 000 = 35 000 cm².

15. A student is measuring tiles. Each tile is a square with side 20
    cm.\
    How many tiles are needed to cover 1 m²? Show your working using
    area, not counting

Area of 1 tile = 20 × 20 = 400 cm²

Area of 1 m² = 10 000 cm²

Number of tiles = 10 000 ÷ 400 = 25 tiles

16. The conversion factor from m to km is ÷ 1 000.\
    Explain why the conversion factor from m² to km² is not ÷ 1 000.

Because area involves two dimensions.

A square of 1 km side contains 1 000 × 1 000 = 1 000 000 m².

So 1 km² = 1 000 000 m², and the conversion factor is ÷ 1 000 000 = ÷
1000².

17. A rural property is rectangular in shape with sides 350 m and 500 m.

    a.  Find its area in square metres and then in hectares.

175 000 m^2^ = 17.5 ha

b.  The owner wants to plant 8 mangroves per ha, how many trees should
    be planted?

140 trees

18. A bathroom floor measures 2.4 m by 1.8 m. Square tiles have side
    length 30 cm.

    a.  Find the area of the floor in cm².

    b.  Find the area of one tile in cm².

    c.  How many tiles are needed?

240 × 180 = 43 200 cm²

30 × 30 = 900 cm²

43 200 ÷ 900 = **48 tiles**

19. A national park covers 45 km².

    a.  Convert to hectares.

    b.  Convert to m².

    c.  A ranger says "I patrol about 500 ha each day."\
        How many days would it take to patrol the entire park?

45 × 100 = 4 500 ha

45 × 1 000 000 = 45 000 000 m²

4 500 ÷ 500 = **9 days**

Mastery

20. A suburban home is advertised as 650 m².

    a.  Express this in hectares.

    b.  A nearby farm is 3.2 ha. How many suburban homes could fit on
        the farm?

650 ÷ 10 000 = 0.065 ha

3.2 ha = 32 000 m². 32 000 ÷ 650 ≈ **49 blocks** (49.2... so 49 complete
blocks)

21. A football oval has area 18 000 m².

    a.  Express in ha.

    b.  A groundskeeper applies fertiliser at a rate of 1 bag per 500
        m². How many bags are needed?

    c.  Each bag costs \$12. Find the total cost.

18 000 ÷ 10 000 = 1.8 ha

18 000 ÷ 500 = 36 bags

36 × \$12 = **\$432**

22. Rebecca is tiling her kitchen floor. The floor is a rectangle,
    measuring 8 m by 6 m.\
    Each tile is a square measuring 20 cm by 20 cm.\
    The tiles are sold in boxes of 10 and each box costs \$8.50.\
    Work out the total cost of the tiles needed for the kitchen floor.

Area of kitchen = 48 m^2^ = 480 000 cm^2^

Area of tile = 400 cm^2^

Number of tiles needed = $480\ 000 \div 400 = 1200$

Number of boxes needed = $120$

Total cost = $120 \times \$ 8.50 = \$ 1020$
