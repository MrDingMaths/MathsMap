# Sketching Cubics in Factored Form

- A cubic function in factored form is $P(x) = k(x - a)(x - b)(x - c)$

> The $x$-intercepts are at $x = a,\ \ b,\ \ c$

1.  Determine curve behaviour as $x \rightarrow \infty$.

    - If the coefficient of $x^{3}$ is positive, then as
      $x \rightarrow \infty$, $y \rightarrow \infty$.

    - If the coefficient of $x^{3}$ is negative, then as
      $x \rightarrow \infty$, $y \rightarrow - \infty$.

2.  Determine the $y$-intercept by letting $x = 0$.

3.  Determine the $x$-intercepts by letting $y = 0$.

# Graphs of Polynomials

## The Leading Term and Constant Term

![](media/Polynomials_4 Graph polynomials/media/image23.png){width="5.088888888888889in"
height="1.5729166666666667in"}

- The **leading term** determines the end behaviour of the graph.

+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Leading Term Positive**                                                                | **Leading Term Negative**                                                                |
+:========================================================================================:+:========================================================================================:+
| As $x \rightarrow \infty$, $y \rightarrow \infty$                                        | As $x \rightarrow \infty$, $y \rightarrow - \infty$                                      |
|                                                                                          |                                                                                          |
| ![](media/Polynomials_4 Graph polynomials/media/image24.png){width="2.463721566054243in" | ![](media/Polynomials_4 Graph polynomials/media/image25.png){width="2.373611111111111in" |
| height="2.3622047244094486in"}                                                           | height="2.3618055555555557in"}                                                           |
+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+

- The behaviour as $x \rightarrow - \infty$ will depend on the degree of
  the leading term.

- The **constant term** is the $y$-intercept.

- For a **factored polynomial:**

  - Find the leading term by multiplying the highest power terms from
    each factor.

  - Find the constant term by multiplying all constant terms from each
    factor.

## Sketching Polynomials

- A polynomial in factored form is
  $P(x) = k(x - a)(x - b)(x - c)(x - d)\ldots$

> The $x$-intercepts are at $x = a,\ \ b,\ \ c,\ \ d$ ...

1.  Determine curve behaviour at $x \rightarrow \infty$ (identify
    leading coefficient).

    - If leading coefficient is positive, then $x \rightarrow \infty$,
      $y \rightarrow \infty$

    - If leading coefficient is negative, then $x \rightarrow \infty$,
      $y \rightarrow - \infty$

2.  Determine the $y$-intercept by letting $x = 0$ (multiply constant
    terms in each factor).

3.  Determine the $x$-intercepts by letting $y = 0$ (use null-factor
    law).

  at the $x$-intercepts.

- For example, if a polynomial has a double root at $x = 5$, also called
  **root of multiplicity 2**, then the curve "bounces" off the $x$-axis
  like a parabola.
