# Inequalities

- $x > 3$ means "$x$ is greater than 3."

- We show inequalities on a number line using:

  - Arrows pointing right for larger values.

  - Arrows pointing left for smaller values.

  - An open circle (○) for \"less than\" ($<$) or \"greater than\"
    ($>$).

  - A closed circle (●) for \"less than or equal to\" ($\leq$) or
    \"greater than or equal to\" ($\geq$).

## Solving Inequalities

- Inequalities have **infinite solutions**.\
  For example, $x > 3$ could be $x = 3.1,\ 4,\ 792$, but not $3$, $2$ or
  $- 4$.

<!-- -->

- When we solve an inequality, we solve for the **solution set**.

- Solve inequalities the same way as equations: apply inverse operations
  to both sides.

1.  Isolate the pronumeral.

2.  Write the pronumeral on the LHS.\
    If you swap LHS and RHS, flip the inequality symbol.
