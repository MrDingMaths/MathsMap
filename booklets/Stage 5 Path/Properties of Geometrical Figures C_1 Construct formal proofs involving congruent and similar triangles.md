$$x + 2 = x^{2}$$

$$x^{2} - x - 2 = 0$$

14. In this figure, ΔABD, ΔCBA and ΔCAD are right angled.

    a.  Prove ΔABD \|\|\| ΔCBA. Hence, prove AB² = CB × BD.

    b.  Prove ΔABD \|\|\| ΔCAD. Hence, prove AD² = CD × BD.

    c.  ![](media/Properties of Geometrical Figures C_1 Construct formal proofs involving congruent and similar triangles/media/image45.png){width="1.8911909448818898in"
        height="1.6487303149606298in"}Hence, prove Pythagoras\' theorem
        AB² + AD² = BD².

∠BAD = ∠BCA = 90°

∠ABD = ∠CBA (common)

So ΔABD \|\|\| ΔCBA (AAA).

Therefore, AB/CB = BD/AB.

AB² = CB × BD

∠BAD = ∠ACD = 90°

∠ADB = ∠CDA (common)

So ΔABD \|\|\| ΔCAD (AAA)

Therefore, AD/CD = BD/AD.

AD² = CD × BD

Adding the two equations:

AB² + AD² = CB × BD + CD × BD

= BD(CB + CD)

= BD × BD

= BD²
