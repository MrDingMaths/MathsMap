# Overview

## Syllabus Content

**MST-11-04** solves financial problems involving budgeting and
purchasing

**Purchasing goods**

- Apply percentage increase and decrease to determine the cost of goods,
  and profit and loss

- Calculate the goods and service tax (GST) or value-added tax (VAT)
  when purchasing goods and services in Australia and internationally

- Solve problems involving GST or VAT

- Examine payment options involving *buy now, pay later* and analyse the
  costs associated with these schemes for purchasing goods

- Calculate the cost of buying on terms that involve paying an initial
  deposit and making regular repayments

- Compare the total cost of using different methods of purchasing goods
  and justify which method of purchasing is best suited to a given
  context

- Calculate the cost of purchasing a vehicle by considering the purchase
  price or loan repayments, registration, compulsory and non-compulsory
  third-party insurance, comprehensive insurance and stamp duty at
  current rates

# Change as a Percentage of the Original

## Expressing Change as a Percentage of the Original

+----------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Percentage Change Formula**                                  |
+================================================================+
| > $${Change = Final - Original                                 |
| > }{Percentage\ Change = \frac{change}{original} \times 100}$$ |
+----------------------------------------------------------------+

  **original** value.

1.  Calculate the change.

    - If the percentage change is positive, then there has been an
      increase.

  made a profit.

## Stamp Duty

- You pay **stamp duty** to the government when buying any vehicle.

- Stamp duty is calculated on the market value of the vehicle or the
  purchase price, whichever is higher.

- **3%** of the market value **up to \$45 000** plus **5%** of the value
  **over \$45 000**.

> A vehicle \$45 000 or under: $Stamp\ Duty = 3\% \times Market\ value$
>
> A vehicle over \$45 000: $Excess = Market\ value - \$ 45\ 000$
>
> $Stamp\ Duty = 3\% \times 45\ 000 + 5\% \times Excess$

# Car Insurance

## Types of Insurance

- **Compulsory Third-Party Insurance** (CTP) is mandatory for all
  registered vehicles. It pays compensation if your driving causes
  injury or death to others.

- **Third-Party Property Insurance** covers damage you cause to other
  people\'s cars, but not your own.

- **Comprehensive Insurance** protects your car and others\' vehicles.
  It typically covers theft and weather damage, with benefits varying by
  insurer.

## Premiums and Excesses

- You pay yearly **insurance premiums** to maintain your coverage.

- When claiming insurance, you pay a one-off **excess** payment.

- Your premiums and excess costs depend on your age, your driving
  history, your vehicle, etc.

- Safe drivers can earn a **no-claim bonus**, reducing their premium
  when they avoid making claims. A no-claim bonus of 10% means your
  premiums are discounted by 10%.
