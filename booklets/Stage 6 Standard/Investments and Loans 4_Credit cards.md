# Credit Cards

## Credit Cards

- **Debit cards** use your own money from your savings account.

- **Credit cards** use the bank's money, which they charge interest for.

- A monthly **statement** keeps track of the spending.

- The cardholder must make a **minimum repayment** every month, usually
  a small percentage of the amount owed.

- Interest on a credit card is **usually** **compounded daily**.

- Compounding **usually** **includes the day of purchase and day of
  payment**.

- Many cards have an **interest-free period**, usually 55 days. If the
  amount is not repaid in 55 days, then interest is charged from the
  first day.

- A **cash advance** refers to withdrawing cash from a credit card
  account. Cash advances usually have a higher interest rate.

- Credit cards are an example of a reducing balance loan; however, there
  usually isn't a fixed repayment.

## Credit Card Fees

- Annual card fee: for having a credit card account.

- Interest charge: for making purchases and not repaying within the
  interest-free period.

- Late payment fee: for missing the due date for a minimum payment.

- Cash advances: for withdrawing cash from the credit card account.

- Balance transfers: for moving debt between different accounts.

- Foreign transaction: for exchanging foreign currency (e.g., paying for
  online purchases).

- Some cards waive fees. (e.g., travel cards)

- **Since every bank and every card is different, the question will
  always provide any required information.**

## Calculating Closing Balance

1.  Calculate daily interest rate.

2.  Calculate how many days between purchase date and repayment
    date/closing balance day.

3.  Treat each purchase as a separate compound interest calculation.
