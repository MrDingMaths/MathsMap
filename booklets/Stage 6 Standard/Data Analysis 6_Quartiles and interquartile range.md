# Quartiles

- **Quartiles** split ordered data into four equal groups.

![](media/Data Analysis 6_Quartiles and interquartile range/media/image3.png){width="1.9511603237095363in"
height="1.0406189851268592in"}

- $Q_{1}$ **First quartile** (lower quartile) 25% of data below this
  number

- $Q_{2}$ **Second quartile** (median) 50% of data below this number

- $Q_{3}$ **Third quartile** (upper quartile) 75% of data below this
  number

To find the quartiles:

1.  $Q_{2}$: Find the median. The median splits the dataset into two
    halves.

2.  $Q_{1}$: Find the median of the lower half.

3.  $Q_{3}:$ Find the median of the upper half.

# Five-Number Summary and Interquartile Range

- The **five-number summary** gives an overview of the data.

+-------------------------------------------------------+
| **Five-number Summary**                               |
+:=======:+:=========:+:=========:+:=========:+:=======:+
| Min     | $$Q_{1}$$ | $$Q_{2}$$ | $$Q_{3}$$ | Max     |
+---------+-----------+-----------+-----------+---------+

- **Outliers** greatly affect range and standard deviation.

<!-- -->

- The **interquartile range** (IQR) measures spread with less impact
  from outliers.

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **Interquartile Range**                                                                                   |
+===========================================================================================================+
| ![](media/Data Analysis 6_Quartiles and interquartile range/media/image7.png){width="1.828358486439195in" |
| height="1.0970155293088364in"}                                                                            |
|                                                                                                           |
| $$IQR = Q_{3} - Q_{1}$$                                                                                   |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
