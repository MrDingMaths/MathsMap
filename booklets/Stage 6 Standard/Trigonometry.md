## The Trigonometric Ratios

- There are three **trigonometric ratios: sine, cosine, and tangent,**
  which compare the lengths of two sides of a triangle.

- The ratios specify an angle, as the opposite and adjacent sides depend
  on the angle.

+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **The Trigonometric Ratios**                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
+======================================================================================+======================================================================================+========================================================================+
| $${\mathbf{s}in}\theta = \frac{\mathbf{O}pposite\ \ \ \ \ \ }{\mathbf{H}ypotenuse}$$ | $${\mathbf{c}os}\theta = \frac{\mathbf{A}djacent\ \ \ \ \ \ }{\mathbf{H}ypotenuse}$$ | $${\mathbf{t}an}\theta = \frac{\mathbf{O}pposite}{\mathbf{A}djacent}$$ |
+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+------------------------------------------------------------------------+

1.  Identify the sides of the right-angled triangle: H, A, O

2.  Write the trig ratios.

## Calculating the Value of an Angle

- Given an angle, you can use trigonometry to find the ratio of two
  sides of a triangle.

<!-- -->

- The reverse is also true: given a trigonometric ratio, you can find
  the angle.

- To do so, use the **inverse** trigonometric function:

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Inverse Sine**                                     **Inverse Cosine**                                   **Inverse Tangent**
  ---------------------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------
  $${\sin\theta = \frac{O}{H}                          $${\cos\theta = \frac{A}{H}                          $${\tan\theta = \frac{O}{A}
  }{\theta = \sin^{- 1}\left( \frac{O}{H} \right)}$$   }{\theta = \cos^{- 1}\left( \frac{A}{H} \right)}$$   }{\theta = \tan^{- 1}\left( \frac{O}{A} \right)}$$

  --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- $\sin^{- 1}{(0.4369)}$ is read "sine inverse 0.4369" and means "the
  angle with a sine ratio of 0.4369"

## Rounding in Sexagesimal Form

1.  Draw a "cut" after the required unit.

2.  Circle the **critical unit** right after the cut.

3.  Decide to round up to down.

    i.  If critical unit is less than 30, round down.

    ii. If critical unit is 30 or more, round up.

4.  Round.

    i.  rounding down: Remove digits after the cut.

    ii. rounding up: Add 1 to digit before the cut, then remove digits
        after the cut.

- **Bearings** give a travel direction from one point to another.

- With bearings, we are always taking a **top-down view**.

- **True bearing** measures the angle to turn clockwise from north.

- True bearings **always use three digits**.\
  If a navigator only hears two digits, then they know they need it to
  be repeated.

10. **2021 HSC Standard 2 Band 5**

Triangle COB has an area of 466 m²

a.  ![A picture containing line, diagram Description automatically
    generated](media/Trigonometry/media/image344.png){width="3.5946095800524933in"
    height="3.454756124234471in"}Find the size of acute angle COB,\
    correct to the nearest degree. 

$$A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$$

$$466 = \frac{1}{2}(28)(35)\sin{\angle COB}$$

$$\angle COB = \sin^{- 1}\left( \frac{466}{\frac{1}{2}(28)(35)} \right)$$

= 72°

b.  A farmer wants to put a fence around the triangle DOC.

> Show that the length of fencing required is 106.8 m.

$$\angle DOC = 330 - (150 + 72) = 108{^\circ}$$

$$DC = \sqrt{31^{2} + 28^{2} - 2(31)(28)\cos{108{^\circ}}} = 47.76\ m$$

$\therefore$ Fence length = $31 + 28 + 47.76 \approx 106.8$ m
