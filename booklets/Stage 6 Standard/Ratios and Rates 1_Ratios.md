## Simplifying Ratios

- A **simplified** ratio has **no common factors** shared between the
  numbers in the ratio.

- To simplify a ratio:

1.  Identify the **highest common factor** **(HCF)** of all the parts.

2.  Divide each part by the HCF.

## Simplifying Ratios involving Fractions and Decimals

- A **fully simplified** ratio has **no fractions** and **no decimals**.

- **If there are decimals:**

1.  Multiply each part by a power of 10 to get whole numbers.

2.  Divide by the highest common factor.

- **If there are fractions:**

1.  Rewrite the fractions with their lowest common denominator.

2.  Multiply by the denominator.

# Dividing in a Ratio

- In a **part:part ratio**, add the parts to find the **total**.

# Capture-Recapture Sampling

- A common way to estimate a species\' population is
  **capture-recapture**.

> **Tagged in population:** Capture some animals, mark them for
> identification.\
> Release them back into their habitat.\
> **Total in sample**: After a short time, recapture animals.\
> **Tagged in sample**: Count how many are tagged.\
> Estimate **total in population** using equivalent ratios.\
> We assume that:
> $\frac{tagged}{total}\ in\ sample = \frac{tagged}{total}\ in\ population\$

- Steps to solving capture-recapture problems:

1.  Set up a ratio table of tagged : total in the sample and population.

2.  Read the question and identify tagged in population, total in
    sample, and tagged in sample.

3.  Fill in the ratio table and find total in population by the unitary
    method or algebraic fractions.

  notation:** $\mathbf{1\ :\ n}$

# Volume of Rainfall

- Rainfall is measured using a tool called a rain gauge.\
  It is a measuring cylinder that collects and measures the height of
  rain that falls.

- To find the volume of rainfall, multiply the area of land by the depth
  of water in the gauge.

- ![Analogue rain gauge \| TFA
  Dostmann](media/Ratios and Rates 1_Ratios/media/image58.jpeg){width="1.4375in"
