# Introduction to Rates

- **Ratio**: Compares quantities with the same units (e.g., student :
  teacher).

- **Rate**: Compares quantities with different units (e.g., distance /
  time).

- Rates show a division of one quantity by another.

- Always include **units** in rates.

- The slash (/) means \"per\" or \"for each\".

- Example: 40 km/h = \"40 km per hour\" or 40 km for each hour.

- ![Text Description automatically
  generated](media/Ratios and Rates 2_Rates/media/image2.png){width="1.3653849518810148in"
  height="0.509076990376203in"}Write rates as **fractions** for clarity.

- These fractions show division, not parts of a whole.

- Always write the **units**.

  second quantity is 1.

# Understanding Units of Energy

## Watts and Kilowatts

- Electricity is a form of energy.

- Power is the rate at which an appliance uses electricity, measured in
  watts (W).

- 1 watt is 1 joule per second.

- For example, a USB phone charger plugged into a computer can deliver
  2.5 W of energy, while a "fast charger" plugged into a wall outlet can
  deliver up to 100 W.

- 1 kiloWatt (kW) $=$ 1000 Watts.

# Related Rates

- Usually when working with rates, we only use two units.

- Sometimes a question will give a *third* unit.

- To solve these problems:

1.  Identify the three quantities in the question.

2.  Set up a **related rate equation**.

> $$\frac{Quantity\ 1}{Quantity\ 2} = \frac{Quantity\ 1}{Quantity\ 3} \times \frac{Quantity\ 3}{Quantity\ 2}$$

3.  Substitute given information.\
    The scenario will always give two of the rates.

4.  Convert units to be the same, and to the unit wanted by the
    question.

5.  Simplify and solve the equation.

- A common application is drip rates for medication, important for
  nurses.
