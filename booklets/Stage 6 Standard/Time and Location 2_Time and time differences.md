  - Ten ones are ten. Ten tens are a hundred. Ten hundreds is a
    thousand...

- Time uses the **sexagesimal** system, based on sixties.

  - Sixty seconds are a minute. Sixty minutes are an hour.

# 12 and 24 Hour Time

- 12-hour time divides the day into am (mornings) and pm (afternoons).

- 24-hour time has no am or pm and is always written using 4 digits.

# Time Differences

- A **time zone** is an area on Earth that shares the same time.

- Lines of **longitude** guide time zone boundaries. Earth has 24 time
  zones.\
  Each zone spans about **15° of longitude**.

- $360{^\circ} = 24$ hours, so $15{^\circ} = 1$ hour.

- Time zone borders don\'t strictly follow meridians of longitude.\
  They often match country or state lines for ease.

- Each time zone has a number. This shows how many hours to add or
  subtract from **Coordinated Universal Time (UTC)** or Greenwich Mean
  Time (GMT), which is the time at the prime meridian.

- ![](media/Time and Location 2_Time and time differences/media/image16.png){width="7.3498458005249345in"
  height="5.042567804024497in"}All time zones shown are Standard Time;
  we will ignore daylight savings for now.

1.  Identify the time zone of:

+----------------------+----------------------+----------------------+
| a.  Sydney           | b.  Tokyo            | c.  Beijing          |
|                      |                      |                      |
| UTC +10              | UTC +9               | UTC +8               |
+======================+======================+======================+
| d.  Berlin           | e.  London           | f.  New York         |
|                      |                      |                      |
| UTC +1               | UTC 0                | UTC --5              |
+----------------------+----------------------+----------------------+
| g.  San Francisco    | h.  Auckland         | i.  Cairo            |
|                      |                      |                      |
| UTC --8              | UTC +12              | UTC +2               |
+----------------------+----------------------+----------------------+

- The time zone with the greater number is ahead of the other.\
  E.g. Sydney (+10) is ahead of New York (−4)

- The **time difference** is the difference between the time zone
  numbers.

- To find time at a location:

1.  Calculate time difference and determine whether the place is ahead
    or behind.

2.  Convert to 24-hour time and adjust:

- If the location is ahead, add the difference.\
  If the number exceeds 24, subtract 24 and the result is the time the
  next day.

- If the location is behind, subtract the difference.\
  If the number is negative, add 24 and the result is the time the
  previous day.

A basic timeline is useful for visualising the problem.
