# Volume of a Cylinder

- A **cylinder** is a 3D solid with uniform cross-section of a circle.

- The volume of a cylinder is calculated using $V = Ah$, where
  $A = \pi r^{2}$

15. ![A4 Cylinders \| NZ
    Maths](media/Volume 3_Develop the formula for finding the volume of a cylinder and apply the formula to solve problems/media/image32.png){width="3.48411198600175in"
    height="2.8344827209098864in"}Which cylinder would have a greater
    volume? Justify your answer with mathematical reasoning.

Short side: $C = 2\pi r = 210,\ r =$ $\frac{210}{2\pi}$

$$\ V_{curl\ short\ side} = \pi\left( \frac{210}{2\pi} \right)^{2}(297) \approx 1042282\ {cm}^{3}$$

Long side: $C = 2\pi r = 297,\ r =$ $\frac{297}{2\pi}$

$$V_{curl\ long\ side} = \pi\left( \frac{297}{2\pi} \right)^{2}(210) \approx 1474084\ {cm}^{3}$$

$\therefore$ cylinder curled up long side has larger volume.
