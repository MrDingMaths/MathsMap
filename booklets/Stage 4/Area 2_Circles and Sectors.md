# Syllabus Content

**MA4-ARE-C-01** applies knowledge of area and composite area involving
triangles, quadrilaterals and circles to solve problems

**Develop and use the formula to find the area of circles and sectors to
solve problems**

- Develop and apply the formula to find the area of a circle:
  $A = \pi r^{2}$, where $r$ is the length of the radius

- Explain how the area of a sector can be developed from the area of a
  circle $A = \frac{\theta}{360} \times \pi r^{2}$

- Find the area of quadrants, semicircles and sectors, and apply these
  formulas in the context of real-life problems

- Calculate the areas of composite shapes involving quadrants,
  semicircles and sectors to solve problems

9.  A farmer uses a sprinkler that waters a circular area. The sprinkler
    reaches 12 m in every direction. Calculate the area watered, to the
    nearest m².

$$r\  = \ 12\ m$$

$$A\  = \ \pi\  \times \ 12²\  = \ 144\pi\  \approx \ 452\ m²$$

10. A circular swimming pool has a diameter of 4.5 m.\
    Calculate the area of the pool surface to 2 d.p.

$$r\  = \ 4.5\  \div \ 2\  = \ 2.25\ m$$

$$A\  = \ \pi\  \times \ 2.25²\  \approx \ 15.90\ m²$$

11. A circular table has radius 60 cm. A tablecloth must overhang by 15
    cm on all sides. What is the area of the tablecloth to the nearest
    cm²? *(Hint: find the radius of the tablecloth first.)*

$$Tablecloth\ radius\  = \ 60\  + \ 15\  = \ 75\ cm$$

$$A\  = \ \pi\  \times \ 75²\  \approx \ 17\ 671\ cm²$$

12. A circular mirror has diameter 55 cm. A square mirror has side

Square mirror: $A\  = \ 55²\  = \ 3\ 025\ cm²$

No, the square mirror has more glass.

14. Find the area of these circles with given radius. Give exact answers
    in terms of π.

$r\  = \ 5$ cm → A = ............... cm²

$r\  = \ 10$ cm → A = ............... cm²

$r\  = \ 30$ cm → A = ............... cm²

$$r\  = \ 5\ cm\  \rightarrow \ A\  = \ 25\pi\ cm²$$

$$r\  = \ 10\ cm\  \rightarrow \ A\  = \ 100\pi\ cm²$$

$$r\  = \ 30\ cm\  \rightarrow \ A\  = \ 900\pi\ cm²$$

What happens to the area when you double the radius?

$$The\ area\ multiplies\ by\ 4\ (since\ A\  = \ \pi r²,\ doubling\ r\ gives\ \pi(2r)²\  = \ 4\pi r²).$$

15. You order a 9-inch pizza from a restaurant.\
    After a while, the waiter tells you that they no longer have 9-inch
    pizza bases and will instead give you two 5-inch pizzas instead.\
    He tries to convince you, saying that you will get more since
    $2 \times 5 = 10$.\
    Explain to him why this is not a good deal.

> **Note**: pizza sizes are measured according to the diameter.

9-inch pizza: $\pi(4.5)^{2} \approx 63.62\ {in}^{2}$

2 5-inch pizzas: $\pi(2.5)^{2} \times 2 \approx 39.27\ {in}^{2}$

This is not a good deal as two 5-inch pizzas are significantly smaller
than a single 9-inch pizza

(This happens because the radius gets squared; if you halve the radius,
the area is a quarter, so giving two pizzas won't make up the
difference)
