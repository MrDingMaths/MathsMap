**Parametric form of a function or relation**

- Recognise that a curve may be represented by two parametric equations
  that give $x$ and $y$ as functions of a parameter and that the curve
  may also have a Cartesian equation in $x$ and $y$

- Express linear and quadratic functions and circles in parametric form

- Convert linear and quadratic functions and circles from parametric
  form to Cartesian form

- Graph linear and quadratic functions and circles expressed in
  parametric form

# Parametric Form

- **Cartesian equations** directly link $x$ and $y$ in one equation
  (like $y = 2x + 1$).

- In **parametric equations**, $x$ and $y$ are defined separately using
  a third variable (the **parameter**). Instead of $y$ depending on $x$,
  both $x$ and $y$ depend on the parameter.

- Parametric equations are useful as they track multiple changing values
  at once.\
  They have essential applications in future calculus topics such as
  projectile motion.

# Converting from Parametric Form to Cartesian Form

- To convert parametric equations to Cartesian form, we need to
  eliminate the parameter:

1.  Rearrange one of the equations so that the parameter is the subject.

2.  Substitute the parameter into the other equation.

1.  Rewrite in the form $\left( \frac{x - a}{r} \right)^{2}$ $+$
    $\left( \frac{y - b}{r} \right)^{2}$ $= 1$

2.  Define $\cos\theta =$ $\frac{x - a}{r}$ and $\sin\theta =$
    $\frac{y - b}{r}$

3.  Rearrange to make $x$ and $y$ the subjects.

You could also memorise $x = r\cos\theta + a$, $y = r\sin\theta + b$.

$$\cos\ \theta\  = \frac{x\  - \ a}{r}$$

$$\sin\ \theta\  = \frac{y\  - \ b}{r}$$

Using Pythagorean identity cos²θ + sin²θ = 1:

$$\left( \frac{x\  - \ a}{r} \right)^{2}\  + \ \left( \frac{y\  - \ b}{r} \right)^{2}\  = \ 1$$

Multiplying by r²:

$$(x\  - \ a)²\  + \ (y\  - \ b)²\  = \ r²$$

This is the equation of a circle with centre (a,b) and radius r.

10. **2021 VCE Specialist Mathematics**

A function is defined parametrically by

$$x(t) = 5\cos(2t) + 1,\ \ y(t) = 5\sin(2t) - 1$$

If $A(6, - 1)$ and $B(1,4)$ are two points that lie on the function,
then find the shortest distance along the graph from $A$ to $B$.

$$\cos{2t} = \frac{x - 1}{t},\sin{2t} = \frac{y + 1}{5}$$

$$\cos^{2}(2t) + \sin^{2}(2t) = 1$$

$$(x - 1)^{2} + (y + 1)^{2} = 25$$

$A$ and $B$ are the rightmost and topmost points on the circle, so the
shortest arc connecting them is a quarter of a circle.

$$d = \frac{2\pi r}{4} = \frac{5\pi}{2}\ units$$
