# Overview

## Syllabus Content

**ME1-11-01** solves problems involving inequalities, functions and
their inverses, graphical relationships between functions, and
parametric equations

**Inequalities**

- Solve cubic inequalities where the cubic is expressed as a product of
  linear factors

- Solve inequalities involving rational expressions with variables in
  the denominator

- Solve absolute value inequalities of the form $|ax + b| \geq k$,
  $|ax + b| \leq k$, $|ax + b| < k$, $|ax + b| > k$, where $a$, $b$ and
  $k$ are constants, using algebraic and graphical methods or the
  characterisation of $|x|$ as the distance of $x$ from the origin

# Inequalities with Variables in the Denominator

- When solving inequalities with variables in the denominator, such as
  $\frac{1}{x}$ $< 3\ ,$\
  the denominator is sometimes positive and sometimes negative. If we
  were to multiply both sides by $x$, we wouldn't know whether to
  reverse the inequality symbol or not.\
  Therefore, we multiply by the **square** of the denominator.

<!-- -->

- To solve an inequality with variables in the denominator:

1.  Multiply both sides by the square of the denominator,\
    then solve using quadratic/cubic inequalities.

2.  Solve $denominator \neq 0$ to see if there is an additional
    restriction.
