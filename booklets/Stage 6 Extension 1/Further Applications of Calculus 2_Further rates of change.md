# Syllabus Content

**ME1-12-05** applies calculus to solve problems involving polynomials,
further rates of change, areas and volumes and differential equations

**Further rates of change**

- Develop models in contexts where a rate of change of a function can be
  expressed as a rate of change of a composition of two functions, so
  that the chain rule can be applied

- Solve problems involving related rates of change using the chain rule,
  given the required formulas for problems relating to area, surface
  area or volume

- Describe and examine the graphs of practical situations where the rate
  of change of a quantity is proportional to the amount $Q - P$ by which
  the quantity $Q$ exceeds some fixed value $P$

- Explain that if the rate of change of a quantity $Q$ over time $t$ is
  proportional to the difference $Q - P$ at any instant, then this may
  be represented by the equation $\frac{dQ}{dt} = k(Q - P)$, where $k$
  is a constant

- Verify by substitution that the function $Q = P + Ae^{kt}$, where $A$
  is a constant, satisfies the relationship $\frac{dQ}{dt} = k(Q - P)$,
  and that $Q = P$ in the case where $A = 0$

- Graph the function $Q = P + Ae^{kt}$, where $k > 0$ and $k < 0$ and
  $A > 0$ and $A < 0$, with and without graphing applications, and
  identify any asymptotes

- Use $\frac{dQ}{dt} = k(Q - P)$, $Q = P + Ae^{kt}$ and the graph of
  $Q = P + Ae^{kt}$ for $t \geq 0$, where $k > 0$ or $k < 0$ and $A > 0$
  or $A < 0$, to model and solve problems where a limiting value of $Q$
  exists, including Newton's Law of Cooling and ecosystems with a
  natural carrying capacity, and justify conclusions in the context of
  the problem

Foundation

1.  The side length $x$ of a square increases at a constant rate of 2
    units per second.

    a.  Write down an expression for the area $A$ of the square.

$$A\  = \ x²$$

b.  Find $\frac{dA}{dt}$ in terms of $x$.

$$\frac{dA}{dt} = \ \left( \frac{dA}{dx} \right)\left( \frac{dx}{dt} \right) = \ 2x(2) = \ 4x$$

c.  Hence, find the rate that the area is increasing when $x\  = \ 5$.

When x = 5: $\frac{dA}{dt}$ = 4(5) = 20 units²/s

2.  The side length $x$ of a cube increases at a constant rate of 3
    units per second.\
    Find the rate of change of the following when $x\  = \ 2$.

    a.  Surface area.

Surface area $S\  = \ 6x²$

$$\frac{dS}{dx} = \ 12x$$

$$\frac{dS}{dt} = \ 12x(3) = \ 36x$$

$$When\ x\  = \ 2:\frac{dS}{dt} = \ 36(2) = \ 72\ units²/s$$

b.  Volume.

Volume $V\  = \ x³$

$$\frac{dV}{dx} = \ 3x^{2}$$

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 3x^{2}(3) = \ 9x^{2}$$

$$When\ x\  = \ 2:\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 9(2)^{2} = \ 36\ units³/s$$

3.  A square metal sheet is being heated such that its length is
    expanding at a uniform rate of 0.05 cm/s. Find the rate of change of
    its area at the instant when the length is 4 cm.

$$A\  = \ x^{2},\frac{dx}{dt} = \ 0.05$$

$$\frac{dA}{dt} = \ 2x\left( \frac{dx}{dt} \right) = \ 2x(0.05) = \ 0.1x$$

$$When\ x\  = \ 4:\frac{dA}{dt}\  = \ 0.1(4) = \ 0.4\ cm²/s\ $$

4.  A circular plate of radius r is heated so that the area of the plate
    is increasing at a rate of 3 cm²/min. At what rate is the radius
    increasing when $r$ = 5 cm?

$$A\  = \ \pi r^{2},\frac{dA}{dt} = \ 3$$

$$\frac{dA}{dt} = \ 2\pi r\left( \frac{dr}{dt} \right) = \ 3$$

$$\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{3}{2\pi r}$$

$$When\ r\  = \ 5:\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{3}{10\pi}\ cm/min$$

5.  A spherical balloon is being inflated by a pump at a constant rate
    of $8\pi$ cm³/min.

    a.  Find an expression in terms of $r$ for the rate at which the
        radius of the balloon changes.

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 4\pi r^{2}\left( \frac{dr}{dt} \right) = \ 8\pi$$

$$\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{2}{r^{2}}$$

b.  Find the rate at which the radius of the balloon is changing when
    the radius is 4 cm.

$$When\ r\  = \ 4:\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{2}{16} = \frac{1}{8}\ cm/min$$

c.  Find the rate at which the radius of the balloon is changing when
    the volume is $36\pi$ cm³.

$$When\ V\  = \ 36\pi:\ \left( \frac{4}{3} \right)\pi r^{3} = \ 36\pi$$

$$r^{3} = \ 27,\ so\ r\  = \ 3$$

$$\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{2}{9}\ cm/min$$

6.  A spherical snowball is melting in such a way that its radius is
    decreasing at a rate of 0.5 cm/min. At what rate is the volume of
    the snowball decreasing when the radius is 3 cm?

Give your answer in exact form.

$$V\  = \ \left( \frac{4}{3} \right)\pi r^{3},\frac{dr}{dt} = \  - 0.5$$

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 4\pi r^{2}( - 0.5) = \  - 2\pi r^{2}$$

$$When\ r\  = \ 3:\frac{dV}{dt} = \  - 2\pi(9) = \  - 18\pi\ cm³/min$$

7.  The radius of the base of a cylinder is increasing at a rate of 6
    cm/min.\
    The height of the cylinder is fixed at 20 cm.\
    Find the rate of change of the volume of the cylinder at the instant
    when the radius is 15 cm.

$$V\  = \ \pi r^{2}h\  = \ 20\pi r^{2},\frac{dr}{dt} = \ 6$$

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 40\pi r\left( \frac{dr}{dt} \right) = \ 40\pi r(6) = \ 240\pi r$$

$$When\ r\  = \ 15:\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 240\pi(15) = \ 3600\pi\ cm³/min$$

8.  The height of a cone is 12 cm and remains constant whilst the radius
    of the base is increasing at a rate of 6 cm/s. At what rate is the
    volume of the cone increasing at the instant when the radius is 10
    cm?

$$V\  = \ \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)\pi r^{2}h\  = \ 4\pi r^{2},\frac{dr}{dt} = \ 6$$

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 8\pi r\left( \frac{dr}{dt} \right) = \ 8\pi r(6) = \ 48\pi r$$

$$When\ r\  = \ 10:\frac{dV}{dt} = \ 48\pi(10) = \ 480\pi\ cm³/s$$

1.  Ten years ago, the population of a certain town was 25000 and today,

    Let the rate of the population be given by $\frac{dP}{dt}$
    $= \ kP$,\
    where $k$ is a constant and $t$ is the time since ten years ago.

    a.  Show that $P\  = \ P_{0}e^{kt}$ satisfies this equation.

$$P\  = \ P_{0}e^{kt}$$

$$\frac{dP}{dt} = \ kP_{0}e^{kt} = \ \ kP$$

b.  Find the exact value of $k$.

$$At\ t\  = \ 0:\ \ 25000\  = \ P_{0}e^{0}$$

$$\therefore P_{0} = 25000$$

$$At\ t\  = \ 10:\ \ 30000\  = \ 25000e^{10k}$$

$$e^{10k} = \frac{30000}{25000} = \frac{6}{5}$$

$$10k\  = \ ln\left( \frac{6}{5} \right)$$

$$k\  = \ \left( \frac{1}{10} \right)\ln\left( \frac{6}{5} \right)$$

c.  Hence, find the rate at which the population was growing ten years
    ago, and the rate that it is growing today to the nearest person per
    year.

$$\frac{dP}{dt} = \ kP$$

$$Ten\ years\ ago\ (t\  = \ 0):\ \ \frac{dP}{dt} = \ k(25000) = \ \left( \frac{1}{10} \right)\ln\left( \frac{6}{5} \right)(25000) = \ 2500\ln\left( \frac{6}{5} \right) \approx \ 456\ people\ per\ year$$

$$Today\ (t\  = \ 10):\ \frac{dP}{dt} = \ k(30000) = \ \left( \frac{1}{10} \right)\ln\left( \frac{6}{5} \right)(30000) = \ 3000\ln\left( \frac{6}{5} \right) \approx \ 547\ people\ per\ year$$
