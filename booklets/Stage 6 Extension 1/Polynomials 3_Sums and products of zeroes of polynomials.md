# Overview

## Syllabus Content

**ME1-11-02** applies the remainder and factor theorem and sums and
products of zeroes to solve problems involving polynomials

**Sums and products of zeroes of polynomials**

- Prove that if a quadratic $P(x) = ax^{2} + bx + c$ has zeroes $\alpha$
  and $\beta$ then $\alpha + \beta = - \frac{b}{a}$, the sum of the
  zeroes, and $\alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a}$, the product of the zeroes

- Prove that if a cubic $P(x) = ax^{3} + bx^{2} + cx + d$ has three
  zeroes $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$, then
  $\alpha + \beta + \gamma = - \frac{b}{a}$,
  $\alpha\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma\alpha = \frac{c}{a}$ and
  $\alpha\beta\gamma = - \frac{d}{a}$

- Prove that if a quartic $P(x) = ax^{4} + bx^{3} + cx^{2} + dx + e$ has
  four zeroes $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$, $\delta$ then
  $\alpha + \beta + \gamma + \delta = - \frac{b}{a}$,
  $\alpha\beta + \alpha\gamma + \alpha\delta + \beta\gamma + \beta\delta + \gamma\alpha = \frac{c}{a}$,
  $\alpha\beta\gamma + \beta\gamma\delta + \gamma\delta\alpha + \delta\alpha\beta = - \frac{d}{a}$
  and $\alpha\beta\gamma\delta = \frac{e}{a}$

- Use the formulas for the sums and products of zeroes to solve problems
  involving zeroes and coefficients of quadratic, cubic and quartic
  polynomials

    show that $p^{2} = 4q + 1$.

$$p^{2} = 4q + 1$$
